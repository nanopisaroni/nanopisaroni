Personal Pantheon

43|

A mental map of 16 thinkers who shaped me

44| 45|
46|

This book is not a summary of philosophy or a list of greatest hits from Western thought. It's something else: a personal pantheon. A collection of 16 people who think (or thought) in a way that made me think differently.

47| 48|

Each chapter is a living profile: who they are, what ideas define them, what works to read to get started, how they connect with others in the pantheon, and — most importantly — concepts you can take into action. It's not meant to be memorized. It's meant to be kept close, returned to, discussed.

49| 50|

It starts with Borges, ends with Naval Ravikant. In between are Feynman, Taleb, Deutsch, Thiel, Popper, Munger, Kahneman, Paul Graham, Eliezer Yudkowsky, the Stoics, Alan Watts, Krishnamurti, and Facundo Cabral. Each is there for a precise reason: they gave me a mental tool I still use.
